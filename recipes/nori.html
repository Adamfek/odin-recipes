Nori recipe japanese

description

Nori is a type of edible seaweed commonly used in Japanese cuisine, particularly in sushi-making. It is a thin, dried sheet of seaweed that is typically dark green or black in color. Nori is cultivated, harvested, and processed into sheets for various culinary applications.

How to Make Nori Japanese

  1. Cultivate nori seaweed in shallow seawater along coastlines.
  2. Harvest the nori seaweed carefully, typically done manually.
  3. Process the harvested nori seaweed by washing, shredding or grinding it into a pulp.
  4. Spread the nori pulp thinly and evenly onto frames or mats for drying.
  5. Dry the nori seaweed in the sun or using specialized drying equipment.
  6. Cut the thoroughly dried nori into uniform sheets.
  7. Package the nori sheets for distribution and sale, typically in sealed packages.
  8. Use the nori sheets in various Japanese dishes such as sushi rolls (maki) or as garnish and flavoring.
